# How do you collaborate with product / design / QA?

**Target time:** 30–45 seconds

---

## Talk track

> I try to get involved **early**, not at handoff.
>
> **With product** — I clarify acceptance criteria before coding. On visa flows at Atlys and internal tools at IQM, I'd ask "what's the edge case for failure?" and "what can we cut for v1?" so we don't discover scope on launch day.
>
> **With design** — I review Figma for **feasibility and perf** — long lists, loading states, mobile breakpoints. I flag when a pattern needs a reusable component vs one-off UI.
>
> **With QA** — I give them **testable slices**: what changed, what to regression-test, known risks. I don't treat QA as "bug catchers at the end" — if I can reproduce it, I fix before handoff.
>
> I also helped **coordinate this through scrum** — standups where product and eng aligned on blockers, sprint planning where we'd negotiate scope together. Again, not a lead title, but I was often the person **keeping the loop tight**.

---

## Three principles (short)

1. **Align before build** — no surprise scope
2. **Share context in PRs** — screenshots, test notes for QA
3. **Close the loop** — post-release, did it work for users?

---

## Avoid

- "I just take tickets from product" — sounds passive
- Claiming you managed product/design — you collaborated and facilitated
