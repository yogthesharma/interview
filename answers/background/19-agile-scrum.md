# What's your experience with agile / scrum?

**Target time:** 45–60 seconds

---

## Talk track

> I've worked in **scrum-style agile** throughout IQM and Atlys — two-week sprints, standups, planning, retros, Jira (or similar).
>
> I wasn't a **Scrum Master** or engineering lead by title, but I **facilitated scrum ceremonies** regularly for our squad:
> - **Daily standups** — kept them short, focused on blockers not status monologues
> - **Sprint planning** — helped break down stories, flag dependencies with backend/design
> - **Sprint syncs / demos** — made sure what we committed matched what we showed
>
> Why me and not only the manager? Our squad was small, I was a **senior IC** who knew the codebase deeply, and someone needed to **keep rhythm** when our lead was in other meetings. I stepped up — voluntarily, not as a promotion.
>
> **How I like to work in agile:**
> - Stories should be **demoable** — thin vertical slices
> - Carry **little WIP** — finish before starting
> - Retros should produce **one real change**, not just complaints
>
> At The company I'd slot in the same way — IC who ships, but also helps the team move smoothly.

## Ceremony cheat sheet (if they probe)

| Ceremony | Your role |
|----------|-----------|
| **Standup** | Facilitated; blockers escalated same day |
| **Planning** | Estimated with eng lens; pushed back on vague stories |
| **Retro** | Participated; advocated for DX/tech debt items |
| **Refinement** | Clarified AC with product before sprint |
