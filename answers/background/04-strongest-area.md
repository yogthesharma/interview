# What's your strongest area — frontend, backend, or full stack?

**Target time:** 20–30 seconds

---

## Talk track

> I'd call myself **full-stack with a strong frontend foundation**.
>
> I spent my early years going deep on **React, TypeScript, and performance** — Core Web Vitals at Atlys, virtualization library on npm, large lists and complex UIs at IQM.
>
> On the backend, **Node/Fastify** is where I've shipped professionally — APIs, JWT auth, React Query integrations. On **Boson** I'm building the runtime in **Rust** — SQLite, HTTP execution, CLI — while keeping the workbench UI in React.
>
> What I'm best at is **owning a feature end to end** — UI, API/runtime, data flow, and making it fast and reliable in production. That's usually where I add the most value, even without a formal lead title.

---

## If they ask "which would you pick?"

> Frontend — that's where I have the most depth. But for this role I'd be happiest doing **both**, which is how I've worked at Atlys and IQM.

---

## Avoid

- "I'm only frontend" — undersells Atlys/IQM backend work
- "I'm a Rust engineer" — honest secondary skill, not primary hire story for this role
