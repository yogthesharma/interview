# Have you worked remotely before? How do you stay effective?

**Target time:** 30–40 seconds

---

## Talk track

> Yes — **Atlys was remote** out of Gurugram, and I've been **fully remote** building Boson and Zyvia.
>
> What keeps me effective:
>
> **1. Over-communicate in writing** — daily updates, clear PR descriptions, documenting decisions so async folks aren't left out.
>
> **2. Own my calendar** — I treated **scrum calls** I facilitated as non-negotiable anchors; prep agendas, keep them short, follow up on blockers in Slack.
>
> **3. Deep work blocks** — mute noise during build time; batch meetings.
>
> **4. Proactive unblock** — if I'm waiting on design or API spec, I ping early, not on deadline day.
>
> Remote works when you **act like an owner**, not someone waiting to be managed.

---

## If they ask about timezone / collaboration

> I've worked with distributed teams before — key is overlapping hours for sync, and everything else documented.

---

## Avoid

- "I need office to be productive" — wrong signal for remote-first companies
